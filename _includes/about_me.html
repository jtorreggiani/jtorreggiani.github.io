About

Studying history and environmental ethics in college impressed on me how far humanity has come, and how far we still need to go to make a world that works for everyone. It made me a critical systems thinker and deeply aware of the challenges facing the world. I saw that that meaningful change requires both understanding systems and building solutions.

After completing my bachelor degree, I went back to school to pursue a medical degree and trained as an EMT with the goal of committing my work to save lives. I switched to engineering because I saw software becoming embedded in every industry, core to the development of sustainable technologies and advanced medicine, and its potential to reach even more people. That realization committed me to a mission of doing the most good I can with my career.

Over the past 13 years, I've worked across edtech, sustainability, and fintech as an engineer, engineering manager, and open-source contributor. Beyond software engineering I've worked as a sustainability advocate, mentor, and champion for mental health and suicide prevention awareness.

Today I run Integrative R&D, a multidisciplinary consulting practice that helps teams and organizations solve complex technical and organizational challenges. I also lead SPEC, an open learning collective I co-founded that provides diverse communities the resources and opportunities to build skills, develop careers, and become leaders of sustainable social impact.
