About

Studying history, philosophy, and environmental ethics as an undergraduate made me a critical systems thinker, impressed upon me the severity of the challenges facing the world, and convinced me that meaningful change requires both understanding systems and building solutions. After completing my bachelor degree, I went back to school to pursue a medical degree and trained as an EMT. I switched to engineering because I saw software becoming embedded in every industry and core to the development of sustainable technologies. That realization committed me to a mission of doing the most good I can with my career as software engineer.

Over the past 13 years, I've worked across edtech, sustainability, and fintech as an engineer, engineering manager, and open-source contributor. Beyond software engineering I've worked as a sustainability advocate, mentor, and champion for mental health and suicide prevention awareness.

Today I run Integrative R&D, a multidisciplinary consulting practice that helps teams and organizations solve complex technical and organizational challenges. I also lead SPEC, an open learning collective I co-founded that provides diverse communities the resources and opportunities to build skills, develop careers, and become leaders of sustainable social impact.
